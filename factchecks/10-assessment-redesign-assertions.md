### SPECIALIST — CONFIRMED
**Sentence:** "Kane's argument-based framework treats validity as a chain of four inferences … Scoring … Generalization … Extrapolation … Decision (Kane 2013)."
**Finding:** CONFIRMED as a faithful rendering of Kane's argument-based approach to validation (Kane, M. **Reference:** Kane, M. T. (2013), *Journal of Educational Measurement* 50(1):1–73.

**Finding:** CONFIRMED. Turnitin CPO Annie Chechitelli, on record: "We estimate that we find about 85% of AI writing. **Reference:** Turnitin (Chechitelli), AI writing detection statements, 2023.

### EVIDENCE / STAT — CONFIRMED (the chapter's decisive ethical finding)

**Finding:** CONFIRMED. Liang, W., Yuksekgonul, M., Mao, Y., Wu, E. & Zou, J. (2023), "GPT detectors are biased against non-native English writers," *Patterns* 4(7):100779. ### EVIDENCE — CONFIRMED
**Sentence:** "Vanderbilt University's public decision (August 2023) to disable Turnitin's AI detector over reliability concerns."
**Finding:** CONFIRMED. Vanderbilt (Brightspace/Center for Teaching) disabled Turnitin's AI detector effective **16 August 2023**, citing reliability, false-positive risk (≈750 of 75,000 papers at a 1% rate), bias against non-native speakers, and the vendor's non-transparency about detection signals.
**Reference:** Vanderbilt University, "Guidance on AI Detection and Why We're Disabling Turnitin's AI Detector" (16 Aug 2023).

**Finding:** CONFIRMED. Corbin, T., Dawson, P. & Liu, D. ### SPECIALIST — CONFIRMED (correctly labeled institutional framework, not RCT)
**Sentence:** "Danny Liu and Adam Bridgeman's two-lane approach (University of Sydney, full implementation 2026; adapted by Auckland, VU Amsterdam, and others — cite as institutional framework, not peer-reviewed causal evidence)."
**Finding:** CONFIRMED. The Sydney Assessment Framework (Bridgeman & Liu) sorts assessment into **Lane 1 'secure'** (supervised, *of* learning) and **Lane 2 'open'** (unsupervised, *for/as* learning, AI permitted with acknowledgement). Published as a two-lane FAQ (July 2024), consolidated March 2025; Sydney targets reforms in place by end of 2026 (the chapter's "full implementation 2026" is fair). Adapted at University of Auckland (TeachWell Digital) and VU Amsterdam. The chapter's explicit "institutional framework, not causal evidence" label is exactly right, as is its inclusion of the over-concentration / equity critiques.
**Reference:** Bridgeman, A. & Liu, D. — Sydney Assessment Framework / Teaching@Sydney "two-lane approach" (2024–2025).
