### EVIDENCE — CONFIRMED at full text (affective misinformation; correct attribution + DOI)
**Sentence:** "Bhat and Long's AIES-25 paper … ('Emotional Plausibility vs. Emotional Truth …', DOI 10.1609/aies.v8i1.36561) … emotional plausibility without emotional truth … affective misinformation … five normative principles … counter-anthropomorphic patterns."
**Finding:** CONFIRMED against the full paper. Bhat & Long (2025), *AIES* 8(1):430–444, DOI 10.1609/aies.v8i1.36561. Every element of the chapter's summary is supported verbatim.
**Reference:** Bhat & Long (2025), *AIES* 8(1):430–444. DOI 10.1609/aies.v8i1.36561

### EVIDENCE / STAT — CONFIRMED (13 preregistered experiments)
**Sentence:** "Schilke and Reimann (2025, *Organizational Behavior and Human Decision Processes*) ran thirteen preregistered experiments, n > 3,000 … actors who disclose their AI use are trusted less … driven by perceived legitimacy violation … robust …"
**Finding:** CONFIRMED. Schilke, O. & Reimann, M. (2025), "The transparency dilemma: How AI disclosure erodes trust," *Organizational Behavior and Human Decision Processes*. Thirteen experiments; disclosers trusted less than non-disclosers across varied actors/tasks; effect mediated by perceived **legitimacy** violation; robust to framing and to voluntary-vs-mandatory disclosure. The paper also notes being *caught* in undisclosed use is worse still — consistent with the chapter's "concealment is not available" move.
**Reference:** Schilke & Reimann (2025), *OBHDP*. (SSRN 5205850)

**Finding:** CONFIRMED. Article 50 imposes transparency obligations including informing natural persons they are interacting with an AI system.
